How It Works

Understanding the science and technology behind our AI-powered predictions

Project Goal

Our Heart Attack Prediction system is designed to build an AI-based clinical decision support system that:

  • Predicts heart attack risk accurately using advanced machine learning algorithms
  • Helps doctors and healthcare professionals understand why a patient is at risk through explainable AI
  • Supports early intervention and preventive care strategies
  • Provides actionable insights for better clinical decision-making

The AI & ML Process

1

Data Understanding & Preprocessing

We analyze medical features including age, cholesterol, blood pressure, chest pain type, and heart rate. Missing values are handled and features are scaled to ensure optimal model performance.

Why it's critical: Healthcare data requires careful preprocessing to ensure accuracy and reliability in predictions.
2

Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA)

We identify key correlations between features and heart attack risk, highlighting the most influential medical indicators through comprehensive statistical analysis and visualization.

Key Findings: Chest pain type, maximum heart rate, and ST depression are among the strongest predictors of heart attack risk.
3

Model Training & Selection

Multiple ML models are trained and compared:

  • Random Forest: Ensemble method for robust predictions
  • Gradient Boosting: Sequential learning for high accuracy
  • Logistic Regression: Interpretable baseline model
  • SVM: Complex pattern recognition
Selection Criteria: The best-performing model is selected based on accuracy, precision, recall, and cross-validation scores.
4

Risk Categorization

Raw predictions are converted into meaningful categories for better clinical usability:

  • Low Risk < 30% probability
  • Medium Risk 30-70% probability
  • High Risk > 70% probability
Real-world Impact: This categorization helps healthcare providers prioritize patient care and intervention strategies.
5

Explainable AI

We identify which features contribute most to predictions, providing interpretable explanations that build trust and transparency in healthcare AI.

Clinical Features Analyzed

Age

Patient's age in years - a primary risk factor for cardiovascular disease

Chest Pain Type

Classification of chest pain (typical angina, atypical angina, non-anginal, asymptomatic)

Blood Pressure

Resting blood pressure measurement in mmHg

Cholesterol

Serum cholesterol level in mg/dL

Max Heart Rate

Maximum heart rate achieved during exercise testing

ST Depression

ST segment depression induced by exercise relative to rest

Major Vessels

Number of major vessels colored by fluoroscopy (0-4)

Thalassemia

Blood disorder test results (normal, fixed defect, reversible defect)
